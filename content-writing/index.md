    <strong>Intro:</strong><br>

  <p>

  </p>

  <p>
    <strong>4. FreeOffice</strong><br>
    <a href="https://www.freeoffice.com/en/" target="_blank">

    Office, thanks to customizable menus and ribbons. Extra features like spelling
    checks, cross-references, and footnotes enhance usability. It also offers
    excellent compatibility with Microsoft Office file formats.
  </p>

  <p>
    <strong>3. Google Workspace</strong><br>
    <a href="https://workspace.google.com/" target="_blank">
      https://workspace.google.com/
    </a><br>
    Google Workspace is a cloud-based alternative consisting of tools similar to
    Word, Excel, and PowerPoint. All you need is a Google account to get started.

    compatibility, templates, and real-time collaboration, it’s a powerful free
    option.

    </a><br>
    Taking the runner-up spot is OnlyOffice, available in both free and paid

    offers an interface similar to Microsoft Office. Since its source code is
    available on GitHub, it’s highly customizable. While minor formatting issues

    </a><br>

    <h2>How David Beckham converted his salary (6.5 million) to 500 million</h2>

    <div class="modal-content">
    <p>
  <strong>Intro:</strong><br>
  20 million dollars in annual salary sounds pretty lucrative, right? You can live
  elegantly by buying luxury cars and villas or traveling to the world’s most exotic
  destinations. But this wasn’t the case for David Beckham. Despite earning 20 million
  dollars per year at Real Madrid, this retired football legend did something that
  literally shocked the world. (Pause)
</p>

<p>
  Young and energetic, David decided to quit Real Madrid and join the Los Angeles
  Galaxy. But here’s the twist—LA Galaxy offered him only 6.5 million dollars per year
  in 2007. That’s roughly a 70 percent pay cut compared to his Real Madrid salary.
  Many people thought Beckham was a fool, but little did they know that this move
  would eventually make him richer than ever—on a massive scale. (Pause)
</p>

<p>
  From his early decision to move to Major League Soccer to the hidden strategies
  behind his football journey, there are many fascinating facts we’re going to
  uncover today. So without further ado, let’s dive right in.
</p>

<hr>

<p>
  <strong>Main Part:</strong><br>
  As briefly mentioned earlier, David Beckham’s move to LA Galaxy was not only one
  of the finest decisions of his life but also one of the most impactful moments
  in football history. He became the first major European football star to join
  Major League Soccer, instantly raising the league’s profile and attracting
  global attention.
</p>

<p>
  At just 31 years old, Beckham viewed MLS as an ideal platform to expand his brand
  and business ventures. Simply put, he saw the bigger picture. His five-year
  contract with LA Galaxy was later reported by The Guardian to be worth a staggering
  250 million dollars, making him one of the highest-earning athletes in the United
  States.
</p>

<p>
  MLS wanted Beckham so badly that they introduced a brand-new regulation called
  the “Designated Player Rule,” also known as the Beckham Rule. This allowed clubs
  to pay one player outside the two million dollar salary cap. However, even this
  rule wasn’t enough to bridge the massive salary gap between Real Madrid and LA
  Galaxy.
</p>

<p>
  To solve this, Beckham and his agent came up with a groundbreaking solution that
  had never been done before in MLS history. Pay close attention now.
</p>

<p>
  The first major clause in Beckham’s contract allowed him to earn a percentage of
  LA Galaxy’s total team revenue. This included sponsorships, merchandise sales,
  ticket revenue, and even concessions. In addition to his 32.5 million dollar base
  salary, this clause earned him an extra 18 million dollars over five seasons.
</p>

<p>
  On top of that, endorsement deals with global brands such as Giorgio Armani,
  Electronic Arts, and Burger King pushed Beckham’s total MLS earnings to an
  astonishing 255 million dollars—an increase of 155 percent compared to his
  earnings at Real Madrid.
</p>

<p>
  The second clause in Beckham’s contract was just as lucrative. He was granted the
  right to purchase an MLS expansion team for a fixed price of 25 million dollars
  after retirement. In 2014, Beckham exercised this option and became the owner of
  Inter Miami CF.
</p>

<p>
  Franchise values in MLS skyrocketed following Beckham’s arrival. The average team
  value rose from 37 million dollars in 2008 to 582 million dollars—an incredible
  1472 percent increase. For comparison, recent expansion teams like St. Louis and
  Charlotte paid between 200 and 325 million dollars just to join the league.
</p>

<p>
  Building Inter Miami wasn’t easy. From 2014 to 2018, five stadium deals collapsed
  due to political and local business challenges. Despite these setbacks, Beckham
  persisted until he succeeded.
</p>

<p>
  Beckham’s influence extended beyond his own wealth. MLS saw massive growth in
  popularity and revenue. The league secured its first TV deal worth 8 million
  dollars in 2006, followed by a 90 million dollar annual deal in 2014 and a massive
  250 million dollar per year agreement with Apple TV in 2022.
</p>

<p>
  The Beckham Rule also paved the way for European stars like Wayne Rooney,
  Thierry Henry, and Zlatan Ibrahimović to join MLS. While Beckham faced criticism
  early on, he ignored the noise and stayed focused on his long-term vision.
</p>

<p>
  His journey teaches us powerful lessons about taking risks, thinking beyond
  short-term gains, and mastering strategic negotiation. Beckham’s 70 percent
  salary pay cut ultimately became the foundation of one of the smartest business
  moves in sports history.
</p>

<p>
  There are even rumors that Lionel Messi may join Inter Miami CF, potentially
  triggering another explosive growth phase for Major League Soccer.
</p>

<hr>

<p>
  <strong>Outro:</strong><br>

  later, he has proven himself to be a visionary businessman. His move to LA Galaxy

    <h2>MSI Claw 8 AI vs Asus ROG Ally X | Which Stays Ahead in Gaming?</h2>

    <div class="modal-content">
    <p>
  <strong>Intro:</strong><br>
  The ROG Ally X and MSI Claw 8 are both powerful handheld gaming devices, featuring
  7-inch and 8-inch displays respectively. But only one of them is truly worth your
  money and time. Today, I’m breaking down their CPU power, real-world gaming
  benchmarks, and overall performance to see which handheld comes out on top.
  Let’s find out who takes the win.
</p>

<hr>

<p>
  <strong>1. CPU Power</strong><br>
  Let’s start with what powers these machines—the CPUs. The ROG Ally X packs the
  Ryzen Z1 Extreme processor, part of AMD’s Phoenix lineup built on the Zen 4
  architecture. It features 8 cores and 16 threads, with a base clock speed of
  3.3 GHz and boost speeds reaching up to 5.1 GHz using AMD’s Precision Boost.
  It also includes 16 MB of L3 cache, helping with faster load times and smoother
  multitasking.
</p>

<p>
  On the other side, the MSI Claw 8 AI uses Intel’s Core Ultra processor. This is
  also an 8-core chip, but Intel divides those cores into 4 performance cores and
  4 efficiency cores. It runs at a base clock of 2.2 GHz and boosts up to 4.8 GHz.
  Built on Intel’s Lunar Lake architecture, this CPU focuses heavily on performance
  per watt and efficiency, with support for Intel Turbo Boost Max Technology 3 for
  optimized core usage.
</p>

<hr>

<p>
  <strong>2. Performance Breakdown</strong><br>
  Now let’s look at performance numbers. In Geekbench 6, the MSI Claw 8 AI outperforms
  the ROG Ally X in single-core tests, scoring 2748 compared to the Ally X’s 2516.
  This highlights Intel’s strength in lighter, everyday tasks.
</p>

<p>
  However, when it comes to multi-core performance, the ROG Ally X takes the lead
  with a score of 12,153 versus 10,468 on the Claw 8. This makes the Ally X better
  suited for heavy workloads like video encoding, 3D rendering, or extended gaming
  sessions that rely on sustained multi-core performance.
</p>

<hr>

<p>
  <strong>3. GPU Comparison</strong><br>
  The MSI Claw 8 AI is powered by Intel’s Arc 140V GPU, built on the new Battlemage
  architecture. With 8 XE cores and boost speeds up to 2 GHz, it delivers solid
  performance for modern games, especially at lower graphics presets.
</p>

<p>
  The ROG Ally X, however, comes with AMD’s Radeon 780M GPU based on the RDNA 3
  architecture. It features 12 compute units and can boost up to 2.7 GHz, delivering
  roughly 8.3 teraflops of performance—nearly double the Claw 8’s 4.2 teraflops.
  This translates to smoother gameplay and better visuals in graphically demanding
  titles.
</p>

<hr>

<p>
  <strong>4. In-Game Benchmarks</strong><br>
  In Callisto Protocol, using low settings with FSR 2, the MSI Claw 8 delivered around
  43 frames per second, while the ROG Ally X managed 34 FPS.
</p>

<p>
  In Cyberpunk 2077 on low settings, the Claw 8 reached an impressive 66 FPS using
  Intel’s XeSS, while the Ally X followed closely with 58 FPS using AMD’s FSR 3.
</p>

<p>
  Red Dead Redemption 2 showed a similar pattern. On low settings, the Claw 8 achieved
  about 64 FPS, while the Ally X came in at 51 FPS. Overall, the Claw 8 holds a clear
  advantage in raw frame rates, though the Ally X still performs well with a few
  setting adjustments.
</p>

<hr>

<p>
  <strong>5. System Memory</strong><br>
  Both devices use LPDDR5X memory, but with some important differences. The ROG Ally X
  includes 24 GB of LPDDR5X RAM running at 7500 MHz. The MSI Claw 8, on the other hand,
  comes with 32 GB of LPDDR5X RAM running at a faster 8533 MHz.
</p>

<p>
  Additionally, the Claw 8’s memory is placed directly on top of the chip, reducing
  latency and improving responsiveness in memory-intensive tasks. This gives the
  Claw 8 a slight edge in scenarios that heavily rely on fast memory access.
</p>

<hr>

<p>
  <strong>6. Design and Ergonomics</strong><br>
  Both handhelds offer distinct designs focused on comfort and performance. The
  ROG Ally X feels premium in hand, with a compact 7-inch screen, solid cooling,
  and a layout well-suited for long gaming sessions. It’s slightly bulky, but that’s
  the trade-off for the performance it delivers.
</p>

<p>
  The MSI Claw 8 features a larger 8-inch display and is slightly heavier due to its
  size and internal components. One notable design advantage is its on-chip memory,
  which helps with both cooling and efficiency. Despite the added weight, it still
  feels sturdy and portable.
</p>

<hr>

<p>
  <strong>7. Final Thoughts</strong><br>
  If comfort and long gaming sessions are your top priorities, the ROG Ally X has the
  edge. Its compact and ergonomic design makes it easier to hold, especially for
  gamers with smaller hands, while still offering strong overall performance.
</p>

<p>
  The MSI Claw 8, however, delivers a larger screen, more memory, and stronger CPU
  performance. At 795 grams, it’s heavier and less portable, but it compensates with
  smoother performance in demanding games thanks to its Intel architecture.
</p>

<p>
  Battery life between the two devices is fairly similar, so the decision ultimately
  comes down to preference. If you want a bigger screen and more raw power, the MSI
  Claw 8 is the better choice. But if comfort, portability, and relaxed long-term
  gameplay matter more to you, the ROG Ally X is the smarter pick.
</p>

<p>
  So, which handheld do you think is better? Let us know your thoughts.
</p>

    <h2>Gastric Chest Pain: Know The Right Cure, Causes, and Symptoms</h2>

    <div class="modal-content">

    <p>
  <strong>Gastric Chest Pain: Know The Right Cure, Causes, and Symptoms</strong><br>
  We all have experienced stomach pain at some point, but when it starts causing
  chest pain, it can be alarming. Gastric chest pain can arise from digestive
  problems, food poisoning, gallbladder disease, or other health conditions. If
  you are experiencing gas-related chest pain for a prolonged time, it’s important
  to take it seriously. Keep reading to learn more about gastric chest pain, its
  causes, symptoms, and possible cures.
</p>

<hr>

<p>
  <strong>Common Symptoms of Gastric Chest Pain</strong><br>
  When gas builds up in the stomach, it can move upward into the chest, creating
  discomfort that often mimics heart-related pain. Common symptoms include:
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Indigestion:</strong> Discomfort or pain in the upper abdomen.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Heartburn:</strong> A burning sensation or discomfort in the chest,
  usually behind the breastbone, caused when stomach acid flows back into the
  esophagus.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Loss of appetite:</strong> Also known as anorexia, this refers to a reduced
  desire to eat or lack of interest in food.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Bloating:</strong> A sensation of fullness or tightness in the abdomen,
  often followed by visible swelling.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Nausea:</strong> A feeling of discomfort in the stomach that may precede
  vomiting.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Belching:</strong> The release of gas from the stomach through the mouth,
  helping eliminate excess air swallowed during eating or drinking.
</p>

<hr>

<p>
  <strong>Causes of Gastric Chest Pain</strong>
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Food Intolerance and Food Poisoning:</strong><br>
  One of the most common causes of gastric chest pain is food intolerance. If your
  body cannot digest certain foods—such as lactose found in dairy products or
  gluten in wheat—it can lead to excessive gas and discomfort. If your body lacks
  the necessary enzymes to digest these foods, it’s best to avoid them.
</p>

<p>
  Even foods that seem safe can sometimes be contaminated, especially fast food
  consumed outside. Such foods may contain harmful bacteria or viruses that can
  trigger stomach issues and chest pain.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Excess Fiber Intake:</strong><br>
  Fiber is generally beneficial for digestion, but consuming too much fiber-rich
  food can lead to stomach gas and discomfort if not balanced properly.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Carbonation and Artificial Sweeteners:</strong><br>
  Carbonated beverages and artificial sweeteners can also cause gastric chest pain
  in some individuals. Drinks like soda and tonic water are common contributors.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Digestive Conditions:</strong><br>
  Conditions such as irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) often cause gas buildup due to
  altered bowel habits. Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) can also cause stomach
  acid to flow back into the throat, creating pressure or gas-like pain in the chest.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Poor Eating Habits:</strong><br>
  Eating too quickly, gulping food or liquids, and not chewing properly can cause
  excess air to enter the digestive system, leading to gas-related chest pain.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Stress:</strong><br>
  Stress can significantly impact digestion. According to Care Hospitals, emotional
  stress can increase gas production and digestive discomfort.
</p>

<hr>

<p>
  <strong>Diagnosis and Tests</strong><br>
  Doctors usually perform physical examinations and diagnostic tests to identify
  gastric chest pain. A standard test includes an electrocardiogram (ECG) to rule
  out heart-related issues. Depending on the symptoms, additional tests may include:
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Endoscopy:</strong> A procedure that uses a flexible tube with a camera to
  examine internal organs.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Stool tests:</strong> Used to detect parasites or signs of bleeding related
  to digestive conditions like Crohn’s disease.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Lactose intolerance tests:</strong> These assess the body’s ability to
  digest lactose found in dairy products.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Abdominal ultrasound:</strong> Helps evaluate organs such as the stomach
  and gallbladder.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Chest X-ray:</strong> Used to rule out respiratory or cardiac causes of
  chest pain.
</p>

<hr>

<p>
  <strong>Home Remedies for Gastric Chest Pain</strong><br>
  The good news is that there are several effective home remedies that can help
  relieve gas-related chest pain.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Water:</strong> Drinking plenty of water improves digestion, reduces
  constipation, and helps keep the body hydrated.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Ginger:</strong> Ginger supports digestion, reduces stomach inflammation,
  and helps relieve cramps.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Exercise:</strong> Regular physical activity promotes healthy digestion
  and helps prevent gas buildup by improving circulation.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Smaller, frequent meals:</strong> Eating smaller portions more frequently
  reduces pressure on the stomach and minimizes gas formation.
</p>

<hr>

<p>
  <strong>Gastric Chest Pain vs. Heart Pain</strong><br>
  Differentiating between gas-related chest pain and heart pain can be difficult,
  especially for first-time sufferers.
</p>

<p>
  Heart pain typically feels like intense pressure or tightness in the center or
  left side of the chest. It often appears suddenly and may worsen before easing or
  developing into a heart attack.
</p>

<p>
  In contrast, gastric chest pain is usually accompanied by symptoms such as
  abdominal discomfort, bloating, flatulence, and heartburn.
</p>

<p>
  If you are unsure about the cause of your chest pain, it’s always best to consult
  a doctor immediately.
</p>

<hr>

<p>
  <strong>Prevention</strong><br>
  Prevention is always better than cure. Maintaining a healthy lifestyle can help
  prevent gastric chest pain. Avoid spicy foods, carbonated drinks, and foods that
  trigger allergies or intolerances.
</p>

<p>
  Pay close attention to which foods cause gas-related chest pain and either reduce
  their intake or eliminate them altogether.
</p>

<hr>

<p>
  <strong>Conclusion</strong><br>
  By now, you should have a clear understanding of gastric chest pain, including its
